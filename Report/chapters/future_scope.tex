\chapter*{Future Scope}
While the presented Quantum-Hybrid RAG model shows promising results, there remain various possibilities for improvement and development:

\begin{itemize}
	\item \textbf{Scalability to Large Datasets:} Future investigations could be oriented towards building a quantum-enhanced framework capable of handling bigger and more diverse datasets, overcoming restrictions associated with the NISQ era in embedding capabilities and generalizability \cite{vats2025quantumnlp}.
	
	\item \textbf{Improved Quantum Circuit Design:} Additional development of quantum circuits based on ansatz design and feature encoding—optimized by means of large language model–guided architecture search—may significantly increase the performance of the quantum algorithm \cite{llmqas2025}.
	
	\item \textbf{Error Mitigation Techniques:}  Improvement of the routing controller in hybrid computation alongside extrapolative error mitigation technique capable of reducing training times will facilitate real-time applications \cite{russo2024layerwise}.
	
	\item \textbf{Real-Time Integration:} Developing an evaluation criterion which goes further than vector similarity by considering measures such as semantic accuracy and structure would enhance the evaluation process \cite{extrapolative2026}.
	
	\item \textbf{Enhanced Evaluation Metrics:} Building a more solid evaluation criteria which involves elements like semantic accuracy and structure beyond vector similarity or n-gram similarity might give us more accurate results for hybrid models \cite{evalvariants2026}.
	
	\item \textbf{Broader Applications:} The two-stage approach used in this paradigm may also find applications in domains where there is complex information to work with and recognize non-linear patterns, such as recommendation systems, signal categorization, and knowledge extraction \cite{radar2025qml}.
\end{itemize}

\bigskip\noindent
The above-described hybrid model is considered a key development in the quest for the successful implementation of quantum-classical architecture in NLP. Given the ongoing developments in the field of quantum computation, these architectural models have tremendous potential to help extract semantic information.

The development of this hybrid system in the future will involve improvement of the delicate balance achieved by the Farm-Fetching concept. With the development of the hardware involved in quantum computing, the sports car aspect of the QRAG system will increase its ability to discover deeper dependencies over greater semantic distance. At the same time, the bike part of the hybrid system will remain an extremely valuable tool for regular retrieval operations. However, the goal of this work does not consist of replacing one approach by another; the goal consists in optimizing the Ambiguity Controller that stands between the two approaches. This controller will allocate the necessary computing resources solely according to the demand.
